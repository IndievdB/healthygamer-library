Common Pitfalls

Trying to do DhyanaA mental state of no-mind — not something you do, but something you enter, the way you fall asleep rather than force yourself to sleep.Full definition → instead of DharanaThe deliberate act of focusing your entire attention on a single object — and holding it there.Full definition →. People sit down and attempt to be peaceful or enter a no-mind state through sheer intention. That's like trying to make yourself fall asleep by staring at the ceiling and commanding yourself to lose consciousness. The state can't be manufactured on demand — only the conditions for it can be set up.

Judging your session by whether you felt the state. If you spend twenty minutes wrestling your attention back to the breath and never feel particularly calm, you might call it a failed session. It wasn't. You did the practice. The state may or may not appear; the practice is what you control.

Comparing your internal struggle to someone else's reported experience. When a seasoned meditator describes floating peacefully through a session, they're describing DhyanaA mental state of no-mind — not something you do, but something you enter, the way you fall asleep rather than force yourself to sleep.Full definition →. When you sit down and your mind is chaos, you're experiencing DharanaThe deliberate act of focusing your entire attention on a single object — and holding it there.Full definition →. These are not the same thing being done at different skill levels — they're literally different phenomena. Stop measuring one against the other.

Believing there's one right technique. Because many different Dharanas can all lead to the same state, finding your technique matters. Some people respond to breath focus, others to sound, others to guided imagery. Getting stuck on a technique that doesn't suit your mind is a fixable problem, not evidence that meditationMeditation is the practice of focusing consciousness completely — until thinking itself falls away and you exist in a state of pure awareness with no mental activity.Full definition → doesn't work for you.
