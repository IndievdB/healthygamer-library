Communication in Relationships

Everyone says communication is the secret to a successful relationship, but no one tells you what that actually means. In this video, we cover both bad and good communication patterns. Bad habits include: getting derailed by analogies (people argue the analogy instead of the point), making identityA stable sense of who you are, built from the narrative integration of emotionally salient experiences — not a fixed trait you possess, but a story you construct by stringing together the moments in your life that…Full definition →-based criticisms instead of behavioral ones ('you're lazy' vs. 'you didn't do the dishes'), getting defensive (which escalates rather than resolves), and stonewalling (mentally checking out, which requires a 20-minute physical break to reset your nervous system). Good communication starts with the little things: positive greetings and partings, daily expressions of appreciation and admiration, and affection. We also cover sharing concerns instead of proposing solutions, developing your own relationship language for recurring patterns, quantifying preferences on a 1-10 scale, and using 'want tokens' to separate what you should do from what you want to do.
