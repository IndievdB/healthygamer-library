Summary

TL;DR — Healthy Gamer's September community event, "Connect the Dots," is a collaborative scavenger hunt with six phases, unique weekly challenges, and rewards unlocked by the community working together.

This is an announcement for a community event rather than a mental health lesson. The details below cover what the event is, how it works, and how to get involved.

How the Event Works

Connect the Dots is a scavenger hunt spread across every Healthy Gamer platform. There are six phases, and the community unlocks them together by finding clues — not by any single person solving everything alone.

Rewards tied to each phase:

  1. New stream emote
  2. New merch
  3. Dr. K dresses as a monk for stream
  4. Dr. K takes bad mental health quizzes
  5. Monk stories
  6. Cooking stream

Clues, Hints, and Where to Look

Hints will be dropped during live events and will not be recorded, so you either need to show up yourself or coordinate with someone who will. Coaches in Social Sandbox will also be able to help, and a detailed schedule will be posted in the Discord.

Weekly Challenges

Each week has a unique challenge — one task that a large group of people needs to complete together for the community to win it. Examples include cleaning, meditating, and journaling.

"You have to do something good for yourself and put together your life to do this challenge."

The Point Is Collaboration

This is explicitly designed as a community effort. The goal is not to figure everything out on your own — it's to work with other people to solve it together.

Common Pitfalls

  • Going solo: trying to complete the scavenger hunt or weekly challenges independently defeats the purpose. Reach out in Discord and coordinate.
  • Missing live hints: because hints aren't recorded, skipping events means missing critical information. Plan ahead or find a community member who will be there.

Action Items & Exercises

  1. Join the Discord and check for the detailed schedule of events.
  2. Visit explore.healthygamer.gg/connect-the-dots to get started.
  3. Show up to live events (or arrange for someone you know to attend and share hints with you).
  4. Complete the weekly challenges — cleaning, meditating, journaling, or whatever that week's task is — and report back to the community.
  5. Collaborate actively: post in Discord, ask coaches in Social Sandbox, and share what you find rather than hoarding clues.
