The Four Paths of Yoga

The yogis recognized that people have different temperaments, and so they described four distinct roads to the same destination:

  1. Bhakti yoga — the path of devotion. What the West broadly calls religion: orienting yourself toward something greater than yourself, praying, worshipping. For people with a naturally devotional or religious temperament.

  2. KarmaThe fundamental principle that every action has a cause and every cause produces an effect — nothing more, nothing less.Full definition → yoga — the path of action. Buddhism and Its Three Branches

The Buddha himself trained in Raja yoga before his enlightenment. Much of Buddhist teaching is grounded in that yogic framework. Buddhism then developed three major branches:

  • Theravada — closest to the Buddha's original words (the Pali Canon). The most conservative branch.
  • Mahayana — recognizes multiple Buddhas and attained beings. This is where Zen originated, and where mindfulnessThe capacity to separate self from mind and observe that gap — what Sanskrit calls sakshi bhav, or "witnessing attitude".Full definition → eventually emerged.
  • Vajrayana — tantric Buddhism, a fusion of the original Tantric teachings with Buddhist philosophy. The tradition of Tibetan Buddhism belongs here.

Later Traditions Worth Knowing

A few more distinct lineages developed over time:

  • Hatha yoga — focuses exclusively on Asana (postures) and PranayamaPranayama is the yogic practice of deliberate breath control used to regulate prana — the body's vital energy — and directly influence the nervous system.Full definition → (breathwork). The claim is that sufficiently deep physical and energetic purification alone can lead to enlightenment — you don't need the ethical or meditative steps of Raja yoga.
  • Kundalini yoga — works with chakras, energy centers along the spine believed to govern different physical and mental faculties. The goal is purifying these centers progressively until enlightenment is reached. Practices around "chakra balancing" that appear in popular culture originate here.
  • Kriya yoga — a more modern synthesis. Where Hatha yoga emphasizes body purification, Kriya yoga draws from multiple traditions to systematically purify consciousness itself.
