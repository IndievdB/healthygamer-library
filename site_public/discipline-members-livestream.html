What Willpower Actually Is (and Why You've Been Misusing It)

Here's the insight most people miss: you don't run out of willpower and then lose the battle. You lose the battle first — internally, quietly — and then the conflict disappears from your mind. The moment you stop monitoring the conflict is the moment you've given up. The relief you feel ("thank God that's over") arrives after you've already surrendered, not before.

That sustained monitoring is the exertion of willpower. You don't have to win the fight. You just have to not lose it. Why Accountability Is a Trap

Accountability only becomes necessary when part of you doesn't want to do the thing. That means you're divided against yourself — and a house divided cannot stand.

The deeper problem: you are not the same person tomorrow that you are today. The person who makes the enthusiastic New Year's resolution and the person who wakes up on January 5th are not identical. Trying to hold "future you" to a commitment made by "past you" is like pursuing a legal case against someone who doesn't exist yet.
