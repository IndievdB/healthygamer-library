If shutting down the HPA axis really does require ~17 minutes, then consistently stopping at 10 minutes may mean you're never fully crossing that threshold — regardless of how long you've been practicing.

Action Items & Exercises

  1. Commit to 20 minutes, not fewer. Set a timer and don't end the session before it goes off. This is the single most evidence-backed adjustment most beginners need to make.

  2. Aim for at least every other day. Five to seven sessions per week is ideal. Treat it like a prescription with a minimum effective dose, because the research suggests that's how it works.

  3. Diagnose which direction you need to move. Before picking a technique, honestly assess your baseline: Are you chronically wired, anxious, and overstimulated? Or chronically flat, low-energy, and unmotivated? Your answer tells you whether you need to calm the sympathetic system or stimulate it.

  4. Try alternate nostril breathingPranayama is the yogic practice of deliberate breath control, used to regulate prana (life energy) and directly shift the state of your nervous system.Full definition → (nadi shuddhi) to directly influence your autonomic state. If you need to calm down, emphasize breathing in and out through the left nostril. If you need to energize, emphasize the right. Even experimenting with which nostril you breathe through is a direct, physiologically grounded intervention — not just a metaphor.

  5. The next time you feel pulled into a negative narrative about someone's behavior — a delayed reply, a short response, a cancelled plan — notice that your association cortices are generating a story. Practice naming the raw event ("they haven't replied yet") separately from the meaning your mind is adding. This is the mental muscle that meditationA practice of focused attention aimed at reaching a state of consciousness outside the thinking mind — where anxiety, desire, hope, attachment, and sadness simply cannot exist.Full definition → trains, and you can practice it off the cushion too.
