Hedonic Circuitry

Sometimes, despite liking something, and even enjoying something, we aren't motivated to do it. Even if we feel good after going to the gym or eating a healthy meal, it doesn't make us more motivated to exercise or eat nutritious food in the future. Overcoming this discrepancy in our behavior involves understanding the difference between the hedonic circuits of the brain - the parts that bring us enjoyment - and the dopaminergic circuits of the brain - the parts that create desireOne of the four classical Hindu goals of life, Kama is the pursuit of pleasure, desire, and sensory enjoyment — including but not limited to sexuality and lust.Full definition →. Due to a disconnect in these circuits, we sometimes don't do things that are good for us, even when we enjoy them.
