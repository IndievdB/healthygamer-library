What works brilliantly for one person may not touch another, and that doesn't mean treatment has failed. It means the formula hasn't been found yet. Variables that actually matter include:

  • Which medication you're on (there are multiple options in both categories)
  • What dose you're taking

  • How long you've given each approach to work

  • Confusing speed of effect with superiority. Stimulants feel more dramatic, but that doesn't make them the right choice for everyone or the most effective long-term option.
  • Getting too attached to stimulants. Because stopping them returns you immediately to baseline, they can create dependency not in a pharmacological sense but in a psychological one. That's a reason to build skills alongside medication, not just rely on the pill.
