Erectile Dysfunction

ED used to be almost exclusively an older man's problem — roughly 5% of men under 30 had it a generation ago. That number has climbed to around 30% in young men today, and pornography-driven masturbation habits are the primary reason.

The mechanism is straightforward: the human hand grips at roughly 100–120 psi; vaginal intercourse provides about 20–30 psi. A decade of daily masturbation literally trains the nervous system to require a level of stimulation that partnered sex can't match. The erection fades before finishing — classic death grip syndrome.

Fixing it means retraining, not medicating: - Use a looser grip - Use lubrication - Cut back on or eliminate pornography

Most young men who make these adjustments see their ED resolve.

  3. Address any mental health diagnosis.
