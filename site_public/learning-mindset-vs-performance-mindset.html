Learning Mindset vs. Performance Mindset

One of the biggest challenges to motivating ourselves is the prospect of failure, and at times, how we handle failure. The psychologist Carol Dweck researched how children can be raised with one of two mindsets: the fixed mindset, where success is determined by inborn traits such as intelligence or talent, or the growth mindset, where success is determined by effort and practice. The way these two mindsets respond to failure is quite different: in the fixed mindset, since failure is determined by intelligence, there's no point in trying again. In the growth mindset, every failure brings us one step close to success.
