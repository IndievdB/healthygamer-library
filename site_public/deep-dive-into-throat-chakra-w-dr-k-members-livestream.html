The Myth of the Churning Ocean

There's an old story from the Hindu tradition about two classes of higher beings — the Devas (roughly, angels) and Asuras (roughly, demons) — who agreed to churn the cosmic ocean together to bring up ambrosia (soma) from the depths. The symbolism here is straightforward: if you leave a pool of water alone, things sink. To bring them up, you have to churn.

When they started churning, the first thing that came up wasn't nectar. It was poison.

Before you get to the good stuff, you work through the bad. This maps directly onto psychotherapy: before breakthrough comes the working-through of traumaTrauma is the experience of an overwhelming emotional event — one where there is simply too much emotion to process in a single moment, forcing the mind to handle the aftermath in waves.Full definition → and pain.

The poison threatened to destroy everything. The beings brought it to Shiva, who took the poison and held it in his throat — neither swallowing it (letting it corrupt him) nor spitting it out (letting it destroy the world). He stored it. This is why one of Shiva's names is Nilakantha, meaning roughly "the one with the blue throat." The practice of the jalandhara bandha (throat lock) connects to this same energy.

The key principle for Vishudha: there is one substance. Purified, it becomes nectar. Unpurified, it becomes poison. The energy itself is neutral. What matters is what you do with it.

The progression (do not skip levels):

  1. Basic (Phase 1): Simple alternate-nostril breathing, no counting, no retention. Breathe in through one nostril, switch, breathe out, breathe in through the same nostril, switch, breathe out. 15–30 minutes per day for 30 days.

  2. Intermediate (Phase 2 — 4:16:8): Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 16, exhale for 8. Ratio of 1:4:2. Same time commitment. 30 days.
