The Confirmation Bias Problem

There's a third layer that makes this even harder to escape. Even when you do take action, Ahamkara will filter the results.

If you go out and don't immediately find a romantic partner, the ego doesn't conclude the data is mixed or this takes time. It says: See? I told you. This confirms it. Any ambiguous outcome gets interpreted as proof of whatever the ego already believed. This is a well-documented psychological phenomenon — confirmation bias — and Ahamkara is its engine.

The net effect is that your sense of identityA stable sense of who you are, built from the narrative integration of emotionally salient experiences — not a fixed trait you possess, but a story you construct by stringing together the moments in your life that…Full definition → doesn't just affect what you do. It shapes what you're able to perceive as true about yourself and the world. It's like wearing tinted glasses: you see everything through the filter, but the filter itself is nearly invisible.

"It shapes our perceptions. It shapes the way that we think about ourselves. And it also shapes our actions."

  3.   4. Notice the confirmation bias in real time. The next time something doesn't go the way you wanted, pause before interpreting it. Ask: Is my ego telling me this confirms something bad about me, or is there a more neutral reading of what just happened?
