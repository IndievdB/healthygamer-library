This is an attempt to build one unified model of existence that makes sense of the esoteric concepts scattered across spiritual traditions: auras, reincarnation, planets as forces, Kundalini, divine hierarchies, Brahman, mantras, and more. The argument is not that these things are scientifically verified — it's that proof and objectivity are features of the physical plane, and these phenomena live on the non-physical plane. Once you accept that both planes are real, everything else slots into place.

The Two Planes of Existence

Start with a deceptively simple question: do thoughts exist?

You can say yes, because you experience them. You can say no, because they're not measurable or scientifically verifiable. Both answers point at something real. Thoughts, emotions, and inner experience are not tangible — they can't be detected in a lab — and yet they are conserved across all of humanity. Every human being has them. The resolution is that they exist in a different plane: the non-material plane, sometimes called the astral plane, the Consciousness plane, the cosmic plane. Different traditions use different words for the same thing.

This isn't a philosophy problem. Science begins with observation. Humans observed genetic inheritance thousands of years before anyone knew what a gene was. The inability to design an experiment that detects something doesn't mean that thing doesn't exist — it means the instruments haven't caught up to the observation yet.

So there are two planes: - The physical plane — the domain of matter, measurable forces, scientific verification, objective truth - The non-physical (Consciousness) plane — the domain of thought, emotion, experience, spiritual phenomena, subjective truth

These planes are not separate universes; they are mirrors of each other. Everything on the physical side has a correlate on the Consciousness side. Your body is on one side; your mind is on the other. A planet exerts a gravitational pull in the physical plane; its correlate exerts a pull in the Consciousness plane. This mirroring is the key to understanding almost everything that follows.

The Spectrum of Spiritual Awareness

Picture all humans as fish in an ocean. Above them is a surface — the boundary between the physical world and the non-material plane. Most fish live too deep to notice it. A smaller group can see faint light coming from above and intuit that something is up there. A smaller group still has actually broken the surface and come back.

Four rough tiers:

  1. The angler fish — completely adapted to the deep ocean. Highly successful in material terms. No conception of, or interest in, anything beyond the physical. They generate their own light (self-made, self-sufficient) and have no need for stars. This is not an insult; they thrive precisely because their attention is undivided.

  2. The surface-dwellers — aware that there's more to existence, though they can't name it clearly. Spiritual but not religious, or religious but experientially uncertain. They can see light filtering down but haven't jumped through.

  3. The jumping fish — have crossed the surface at least once. They've seen the stars and come back changed.

  4. The mud skipper — a fish that can actually survive above the surface. The human equivalent is a yogi: someone who has so adapted their practice that they operate partly outside the normal conditions of material existence.

But even the mud skipper is just the beginning. Becoming able to leave the water is not the endpoint — it's step one. Beyond the surface is the atmosphere, and beyond that is space, and beyond that are galaxies. The yogis in the Himalayas aren't just leaving the water for a moment; they're building rockets.

Psychedelics work for any fish regardless of depth — they're like an elevator. But the elevator ride doesn't make you an astronaut. Sustained contemplative practice does.

How Existence Is Structured: From Brahman to Matter

This is the creation model, built bottom-up from first principles.

Brahman is the starting point: formless, infinite, undifferentiated. It precedes everything, including time. This is what physics roughly calls the state before the Big Bang — a condition of pure potential where time as a concept doesn't yet apply. Yogic texts call this Bindu, and they describe the universe as emerging from it. The two framings are surprisingly close.

Out of Brahman — infinity — comes the first act of creation: finitude. Something finite. The moment you have infinity and finitude, you have duality: zero and one, formless and form.

Once you have two poles, you have the conditions for oscillation. Oscillation requires location and time — you can't be in two places simultaneously, so moving between them introduces a before and an after. This is why vibration and spacetime are, at a fundamental level, the same thing. The yogic framework says the same thing: vibration is the most fundamental unit of manifest existence.

From oscillation come the five tattvas (elements): not the physical elements, but five fundamental qualities of consciousness that correspond to what eventually become earth, water, fire, air, and space in the material world. These don't exist in the physical plane directly; they exist in the Consciousness plane and reflect into matter.

Mantras, Planets, and Other Esoteric Concepts Explained

With this framework, many confusing spiritual concepts become straightforward:

Mantras are vibratory frequencies in the Consciousness plane. The logic: if oscillation is the fundamental unit of existence, then certain oscillations correlate with certain manifestations. Chanting a mantra correctly is working at the level of Consciousness, not the physical level — but because the two planes mirror each other, effects eventually show up in the material world. Planets and astrology (grahas) are not about the physical masses orbiting the sun. In the physical plane, all gravitational influence is the same formula — mass, distance, constant. There's no qualitative difference between one planet's pull and another's. But in the Consciousness plane, there are large bodies of conscious energy that exert qualitative pulls on your mind — not deterministic, but probabilistic. Astrology is where psychology lives in the Eastern framework. The physical planet is the mirror of the Consciousness-plane force, not the source of it.

Asuras are conscious beings at a level above humans but below the high-order gods. They are closer to the material world, which means they can produce faster material results than greater gods who have no particular interest in human affairs. The signature of asura influence: rapid and statistically unusual material advancement combined with paradoxical increasing unhappiness. Common Pitfalls

Expecting proof. The non-material plane is the realm of subjective truth. Asking for objective, falsifiable evidence is like asking a fish to measure the temperature of the sun using only water pressure. You will not find it, and the absence of it tells you nothing. This is also why fraudulent teachers thrive: since none of it can be falsified, anyone can claim to have the secret technique. The answer is to become your own Guru eventually, not to find the one person who can prove it.

Confusing the mirror for the source. The physical planet is not what's pulling on your consciousness. The physical brain is not what's generating your thoughts. The candle is not the fire of spiritual purification. The physical correlate is real, but it's the reflection, not the origin.

Rushing the practice. A lifetime feels long only when your unit of measurement is one life. As your sense of identification with a single body loosens — which itself requires sustained practice — a lifetime starts to feel like a day. Impatience is almost definitionally a sign you're still operating from deep in the material plane.

Mistaking the fruit for the practice. When a mantra starts working, the mind wants to focus on the results. This is exactly when people stop cultivating the plant that was producing the fruit. Keep practicing regardless of what manifests.

Pursuing specific over general. A highly specific mantra (manifest this ring, this job) requires starting over once that particular thing materializes. A broad-spectrum mantra (cultivating the essence of abundance, or connection) keeps accelerating and fills in whatever the next gap is, like irrigation rather than bucket-pouring.

Confusing imagination for experience. If you practice astral travel or deep states for years while wanting it intensely, the mind will manufacture an experience that feels like the real thing. Posting about it enthusiastically is a good sign it wasn't real. Authentic experiences tend to produce more silence than announcement.
