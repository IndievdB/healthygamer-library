Why Men Don't Feel Their Own Anxiety

There are two overlapping reasons:

Biology. Estrogen increases sensitivity to internal emotional states. Women, on average, can feel their emotions more readily. Men's hormonal profile makes that kind of introspection less automatic.

Socialization. Research on parent-child interactions shows that when boys showed fear or distress, parents were more likely to encourage them to confront the stressor. When girls showed the same distress, parents were more likely to comfort them. Boys learn to fix problems. Girls learn to feel feelings.
