Goals Are in the Future; Action Is Only Ever Now

Goals are useful for one specific purpose: organizing action in sequence. That's it. When they grow beyond that function, they start to cause harm.

You Don't Control Your Thoughts — You Control Your Response

This is the most practically important and most misunderstood point in the entire conversation.

You cannot control what thoughts arise in your mind. The mind generates thoughts automatically, the way your stomach digests food. Trying to control your thoughts directly is trying to swim up a waterfall.

What you can control is how you respond when a thought arrives.

"99% of karmaKarma is the principle that all effects have causes and all causes produce effects — nothing more, nothing less.Full definition → happens in here. When you have a thought, how do you respond to that thought? That is technically an action."

Every time you act on an impulse — check the phone, give in to the anxious urge to look at your partner's texts, spiral deeper into a self-critical thought — you're teaching your brain to generate more of that. Every time you notice the impulse and don't feed it, you're letting that weed wither. You're not creating new thoughts directly, but you're choosing which ones get sunlight.
