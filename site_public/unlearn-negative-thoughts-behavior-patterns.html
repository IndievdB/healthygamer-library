Shunya: The Void and What Sits Beneath the Ego

If the ego is everything you identify with, what's left when you peel those layers back?

Shunya (Sanskrit for "void" or "zero") is a meditative concept and practice that points toward this. It's not emptiness in a bleak sense — it's the raw, unadorned experience of being before any narrative identityA narrative you construct by stringing together emotionally salient experiences — the moments in your life that carried the most emotional charge — into a coherent story of who you are.Full definition → gets attached. Most people touch it briefly in ordinary life: watching a beautiful sunset and ceasing to exist as a person for a moment; the relief of finally going to the bathroom after a long wait; the gap between breaths. In those flashes, the mortgage and the inbox and the criticism from the internet are simply not present.

A simple introductory practice:

  • Close your eyes. Breathe in, then breathe out slowly.
  • Rather than focusing on the breath itself, pay attention to the pause between breaths — the stillness between exhalation and the next inhalation.
  • Another entry point: try to catch the precise moment when inhalation becomes exhalation. That transition point is the door to shunya.

The deeper utility is this: when something painful happens — a harsh criticism, a loss, an online attack — the ego and the body receive the hit. But if you have cultivated some access to shunya, you can experience the pain and simultaneously notice that something in you is not the pain. You are the witness, not the wound.
