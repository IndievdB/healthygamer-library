Summary

TL;DR — Healthy Gamer's membership pricing and platform structure are changing; existing members can lock in their current rate before the increase takes effect.

This is a short announcement, not a lesson. It covers why the membership price is going up, what's changing on YouTube versus the standalone platform, and what current members need to do (or can choose to do) before the new pricing kicks in.

What's Actually Changing and Why

When the membership launched, ten dollars a month bought access to a handful of lecture hours. The content library has since grown to over 200 hours and now includes features that didn't exist at the start:

  • Lectures — the original core offering
  • Quests — action steps paired with each lecture
  • Primers — structured onboarding for members who joined later and needed to catch up
  • MeditationMeditation is the practice of focusing consciousness completely — until thinking itself falls away and you exist in a state of pure awareness with no mental activity.Full definition → tracks — a dedicated, structured meditation program
  • Action workshops — hands-on sessions available exclusively to members

Each of those additions was built in direct response to member requests. That expansion is the reason for the price increase — the library and the features are genuinely larger than what the original price reflected.

The YouTube vs. Platform Split

YouTube's infrastructure can't support quests, primers, or meditation tracks, which is why a separate Healthy Gamer platform exists at all. That split creates two practical tiers for existing members:

  1. Stay on YouTube (Tier 2 baseline) — You keep access to all lectures, old and new, through the YouTube interface you already know. The old Tier 1 price point is being retired; Tier 2 becomes the floor.
  2. Move to the HG platform — You get everything above plus the additional features (quests, primers, meditation tracks, action workshops).

YouTube also doesn't allow prices to be changed directly, so the adjustment there is structural — retiring the lower tier rather than editing a number.

The Grandfathering Window

Current members have a window to sign up on the HG platform at the existing rate. Future members won't have access to that rate. If you're reading this and you're already a member, that's the one time-sensitive action item here.

Action Items & Exercises

  1. Decide which experience you actually want. If lectures alone cover what you need and you prefer YouTube's interface, staying on Tier 2 is a legitimate choice. If you want quests, primers, meditation tracks, or action workshops, you need to move to the HG platform.
  2. Lock in the current rate while the window is open. Sign up on the Healthy Gamer platform before the price increase takes effect to keep your existing membership rate.
  3. Check which features you've been underusing. If you're already a member, look at whether quests or meditation tracks exist for content you've already watched — those are the features most people don't know to look for.
