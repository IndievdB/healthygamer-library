Why Porn Specifically Is So Hard to Quit

Pornography is uniquely difficult to address compared to substances like alcohol or heroin, and the neuroscience explains why.

Sexual behavior is regulated by what researchers describe as a full orchestra of brain regions — both subcortical structures (the hypothalamus, brain stem, spinal cord) and multiple cortical areas. Unlike alcohol, which binds to specific receptors that can be blocked by medication like naltrexone, pornography activates essentially every part of the brain and recruits essentially every neurotransmitter system. There is no equivalent of a pill you can take.

That's not a detail — it's the whole problem.
