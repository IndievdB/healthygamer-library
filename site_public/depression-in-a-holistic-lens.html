The Five Layers of a Human Being

Panchakosha (Sanskrit: pancha = five, kosha = sheath or covering) holds that every human being is composed of five nested layers:

  1. Annamaya kosha — the physical body. The tangible, biological you: tissues, organs, neurochemistry.
  2. Pranamaya kosha — the energetic body. Your vital life energy (called pranaYour body's vital life energy — the animating force that keeps you alive, equivalent to qi in the Chinese system.Full definition → in India, chi in China). This is the dimension a doctor can't capture in a blood test, yet everyone recognizes it — the difference between a day when you feel alive and a day when you feel completely drained.
  3. Manomaya kosha — the emotional body. The felt experience of emotions: sadness, joy, anger, shameA painful emotion triggered by the gap between where you are and where you think you should be — essentially, I am not what I should be.Full definition →.
  4. Vijnanamaya kosha — the intellectual body. Critical thinking, analysis, understanding — and crucially, identityA narrative you construct by stringing together emotionally salient experiences — the moments in your life that carried the most emotional charge — into a coherent story of who you are.Full definition →. The story you hold about who you are lives here.
  5. Anandamaya kosha — the spiritual body. The dimension that transcends the other four. This is where meaning and purpose live — the sense that your existence matters and is pointed somewhere.

Why Treating Only One Layer Falls Short

Antidepressant medication works on the physical layer. It can genuinely reduce symptoms — but it leaves the energetic, emotional, intellectual, and spiritual layers completely untouched. That's not a reason to avoid medication; it's a reason not to stop there.

The same logic runs in reverse. Pure talk therapy may reshape your emotional and intellectual layers while your physical body stays depleted — low vitamin D, poor diet, no movement — and your sense of purpose remains absent. Progress stalls.

Sustained remission, or anything close to a cure, tends to happen when all five layers are addressed together.
