Flirting

Flirting is the most basic form of romantic communication, and it's a lost art in our digital age. In this video, we explore the science behind flirting, including research showing that neutral observers only detect flirting about 30% of the time, which is actually by design. The whole point of flirting is plausible deniability: it creates a safe, playful potential space for romantic interest to develop without pressure or commitment. We'll cover why humor is central to flirting (it indicates empathy, intellect, and the ability to read someone), how to develop flirtatious skills through playful social interaction, and why it's okay for your signals to be missed, because sending a soft signal that's missed is better than sending a loud one that overwhelms.
