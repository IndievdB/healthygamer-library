The Cortisol and Adrenaline Connection

A neurotypical brain responds to demands by ramping up. Why Standard ADHD Medication Doesn't Work as Well

Methylphenidate (e.g., Ritalin) is designed to amplify the brain's braking system. That's useful when the problem is a runaway hyperactive brain. It's much less useful when the problem is a brain that won't start.

The medication with better evidence for CDS is atomoxetine, which works primarily on the noradrenergic system — the noradrenaline/adrenaline pathway. This makes intuitive sense: if the core deficit is a failure to activate, something that boosts the body's arousal and activation chemistry is more on-target than something that tightens the reins on an already sluggish system. Stimulants have some role, but they're not the primary lever here.
