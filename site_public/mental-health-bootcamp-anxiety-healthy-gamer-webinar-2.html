The Mind That Feeds and the Mind That Starves

Here's a useful frame: the anxious mind is a living thing that needs food and water. What feeds it?

Ask: am I moving toward a real answer, or am I going in circles? If you're going in circles, stop adding thoughts. Switch to a present-moment activity instead.

  4. Stop seeking reassurance for recurring anxieties.   5. Do one fully present-moment activity today. Eat something slowly and actually taste it. Take a shower and notice only the physical sensations. Walk somewhere and only notice what you see. The goal is a few minutes where the mind has nothing to chew on but now.

  6. When you catch yourself worrying about "potential" or long-term outcomes, redirect to one concrete action you can take today. Not a plan. Not a strategy. One thing. Do it.
