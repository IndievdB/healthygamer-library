Wanting Mode vs. Having Mode

At the core of this is a simple but powerful distinction. The mind can be in one of two states at any given moment:

  • Wanting mode: your attention is on what's absent. I don't have this. I need this. I'm missing this.
  • Having mode: your attention is on what's present. I have this. This is good. This is enough.

These two states produce entirely different emotional experiences — not because your circumstances changed, but because your attentional focus changed.

This is why gratitude practices often feel hollow when people first try them. You can't be genuinely grateful for what you have while your mind is actively cataloguing what you don't have. The two orientations are incompatible. Wanting crowds out having, not the other way around.

The standard solution most people reach for is: get the thing you want. And yes, acquiring something does shift you from wanting mode to having mode — briefly. But that approach makes contentment permanently dependent on external acquisition. The VC firm partner keeps needing new things to move. The next raise, the bigger house, the next milestone. It never stops.
