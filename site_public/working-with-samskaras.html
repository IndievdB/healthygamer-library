The Two Features That Make Samskaras Tricky

When you lay down a memory at age six, that memory doesn't mature as you do. It stays frozen at six. This is why a fully grown adult can find themselves throwing a temper tantrum, or spiraling into a fear that feels completely out of proportion to the actual situation. The emotional experience isn't happening in adult-you; it's happening in the child who first felt that way.
