What Alexithymia Actually Is

AlexithymiaThe inability to recognize or name your own internal emotional state — being, as the framing goes, emotionally colorblind.Full definition → literally means "no words for feelings." It's not that these people feel nothing — the emotions are present and active in the brain. The problem is that the person has no conscious access to them. Think of it as emotional color blindness: the colors exist in the world, but you can't perceive them.

It shows up in four specific ways:

  1. Difficulty identifying feelings — you know something is off, but you can't name what it is.
  2. Difficulty describing feelings to others — even when you sense something, you can't translate it into words another person can understand.
  3. Externally oriented, stimulus-bound thinking — you can only motivate yourself when there's an outside deadline or consequence pushing you. Internal drives are essentially offline.
  4. Constricted imagination — difficulty picturing a desired future. When asked "where do you want to be in five years?" the honest answer is a blank. You can list what you don't want, but you can't picture what you do want.
