How Existence Unfolds: A Creation Model

Here is the architecture that lets the weird stuff fit together — a cascade from formless to concrete, where every layer has a physical mirror and a conscious mirror.

  1. Brahman / infinity. The formless infinite — pure existence, called God or cosmic consciousness.
  2. Form / finity. Out of infinity comes a finite. The instant you have both, you have duality — zero and one.
  3. Dimension and time. Two poles create a dimension, enabling movement and oscillation. But oscillation needs a location and a before-and-after — so it requires time. Oscillation and space-time are the same thing.
  4. Mahakal — time itself. The highest tier below Brahman. This matches physics: time begins with the Big Bang, the moment "before" a single infinite point — what yogis called bindu visarga, "out of which the universe expands."
  5. Om — the first vibration. For the formless to take form, existence must vibrate. Om is the most basic frequency, the origin of the manifest universe — the first oscillation of Brahman.
  6. The elements (tatvas). Harmonics of that oscillation become the five elements — building blocks that exist only on the consciousness plane.
  7. Prana — vital life energy — and primordial gods like Agni (fire), Varuna (water), Vayu (wind), raw forces of nature without human jealousy or pride.
  8. Mind, devas, and asuras. Nearer the matter barrier sit more human-feeling gods like Indra, vain and proud like Zeus.
  9. Brahma — creation bound by time. Brahma is born with each universe and dies with it — the spiritual correlate of the Big Bang, which is exactly why texts call him a lesser god, while Shiva and Mahakal transcend time.

This produces a tier list: S-tier — Mahakal, Shiva, Vishnu (transcend time); A-tier — Brahma; B-tier — Vayu, Varuna, Agni; C-tier — Indra, devas, asuras; D-tier — humans; E — animals; F — plants; G — inanimate matter. Each rung is a layer of manifestation, getting more concrete and more "human" the closer it sits to the material barrier.

A key inversion: the less you can detect something on the physical side, the more profound it is on the conscious side. And science only ever explores in one direction — deeper into matter. The direction into consciousness is just as vast and barely mapped.

Planets, Om, and Other Esoterica

Astrology / grahas. A graha isn't the rock in the sky — it's the equivalent of that planet on the consciousness plane. In matter, every mass exerts the same gravitational pull. But on the conscious plane, vast bodies of "stuff" exert qualitatively different pulls on your mind. Astrology is where psychology lives in the Eastern system — and it is probabilistic, not deterministic. The forces push; you can resist or flow.

Om, decomposed. Chant it slowly and find three sounds plus a fourth element:

  • A (ah) — pure potential, infinity, the source of all sound.
  • U (oo) — reaction, transmutation. This is Vishnu the maintainer.
  • M — finity, the closing of all things. This is Shiva the destroyer.
  • Silence — beyond even infinity. If sound is the first unit of existence, silence is the unit of non-existence — Brahman, the void (shunya).

Karma, Attachment, and Full Response

The more fully you respond, the less karma you accumulate.

Attachment is a restriction from appropriate response.

Picture an overflowing trash can. The complete response is to take the bag out — done, zero residue. The attached response is to keep piling trash higher and tie it off, but never remove it, because you don't want to. That incomplete response lingers.

So the skill is to respond 100% without attachment — not over-responding, not under-responding, not bending behavior around what you cling to. In medicine, do everything appropriate for a patient and fully accept you can't control life and death; get caught up in "saving a life" and you'll do more harm than good. Keep your enjoyment, even keep the action — only give up the attachment.

Spotting an Asura

A practical diagnostic. You can tell an asura (a being just above the human level) is at work by one signature:

Rapid, abnormal material advancement combined with paradoxical unhappiness.

When someone gets everything they asked for and grows more miserable, that's the tell. Asuras grant wishes like a malevolent genie — but the granting never satisfies. That's precisely why you don't work with them.

Common Pitfalls

  • Demanding proof. Proof and objectivity are features of the physical plane. Waiting for science before you'll explore is like an anglerfish refusing to believe in the sun.
  • Treating "objective = real, subjective = fake." If facing a different direction feels different while the brain scan looks identical, the logical conclusion isn't "it's nothing" — it's that there's a third variable outside the brain.
  • Chasing the fruit instead of tending the plant. When practice bears fruit, people fixate on the fruit and neglect the plant. Keep watering it — for years more.
  • Letting expectation sabotage you. Expectation keeps the mind active, breeds disappointment, and anchors consciousness back in the body. The more you want an experience, the more your mind manufactures a convincing fake of it.
  • Mistaking the mudskipper for the destination. Capable meditators plateau at the surface, never realizing it's only step one.
  • Confusing cause and effect. Fertile spiritual terrain fostered the traditions, just as fertile soil fosters farmers. Don't mythologize the people; respect the conditions.
  • Rushing. Progress is measured in lifetimes, unbearable only from inside a one-life frame.
  • Ego about understanding. Grasping this comes from prior moments of confusion that were necessary brushstrokes. Not grasping it is the first stroke on a blank canvas. Neither is better.

A note on patience: exploring this plane "takes lifetimes," and that only sounds daunting because your unit of measurement is a single life. How Existence Unfolds: A Creation Model

Here is the architecture that lets all the weird stuff fit together. It is a cascade from formless to concrete, and every layer has a physical mirror and a conscious mirror.

  1. Brahman / infinity. In the beginning there is the formless infinite — pure existence, which people call God or cosmic consciousness.
  2. Form / finity. Out of the formless comes form; out of infinity comes a finite. The instant you have both, you have duality — zero and one.
  3. Dimension and time. Two poles create a dimension, which makes movement possible, which makes oscillation possible. But oscillation requires two things: a location and a before-and-after. You can't be in two places at once, so oscillation requires time. Oscillation and space-time are one and the same.
  4. Mahakal — time itself. This is the highest tier below Brahman: time, the goddess Mahakal. Notice this matches physics: time is said to begin with the Big Bang, and the moment "before" was a single infinite point. The yogis called that point bindu visarga, "out of which the universe expands."
  5. Om — the first vibration. For the formless to take form, existence must vibrate. Om is the most basic frequency, the origin of the manifest universe — not Brahman itself, but the first oscillation of it.
  6. The elements (tatvas). The harmonics of that fundamental oscillation become the five elements — the building blocks of the universe, which exist only on the consciousness plane, never detectable as themselves in matter.
  7. Prana — vital life energy — and primordial gods like Agni (fire), Varuna (water), Vayu (wind). In the old myths these gods read as raw forces of nature, not yet "human" — no jealousy or pride.
  8. Mind, devas, and asuras. As oscillations combine into more concrete things, we approach the barrier with matter. Here sit gods who feel more human — Indra, vain and proud like Zeus — plus devas and asuras.
  9. Brahma — creation bound by time. Brahma is born with each universe and dies with it. In other words, the spiritual correlate of the Big Bang is Brahma the creator. That's exactly why the texts call him a lesser god: he comes and goes with creation, while Shiva and Mahakal transcend time.

This produces a tier list of gods: S-tier — Mahakal, Shiva, perhaps Vishnu (they transcend time); A-tier — Brahma; B-tier — Vayu, Varuna, Agni; C-tier — Indra, devas, asuras; D-tier — humans; E-tier — animals; F-tier — plants; G-tier — inanimate matter. Each rung is a layer of manifestation with its own consciousness component, getting more concrete and more "human" the closer it sits to the material barrier.

A key inversion: the less you can detect something on the physical side, the more profound it is on the conscious side. Notice too that science only ever explores in one direction — deeper into matter. The other direction, into consciousness, is just as vast and barely mapped.

Planets, Om, and the Other Esoterica

Astrology / grahas. A graha is not the lump of rock in the sky — it's the equivalent of that planet on the consciousness plane. In the material world, every mass exerts the same kind of gravitational pull (just mass over distance). Om, decomposed. Chant it slowly and you find three sounds plus a fourth element:

  • A (ah) — pure potential, infinity, the source from which all sound can arise.
  • U (oo) — reaction, alchemy, transmutation. This is Vishnu the maintainer: the governing of potential as it moves toward closure.
  • M — finity, the end, the closing of all things. This is Shiva the destroyer.

Try the experiment yourself: make any sound with your mouth and you'll find it is sustained by an underlying "uh," and the instant that vanishes the sound stops. The structure is in there.

Karma, Attachment, and Full Response

Attachment is a restriction from appropriate response.

Picture an overflowing trash can. The complete response is to take the bag out and replace it — done, zero residue. The attached response is to keep piling trash higher, tie it off so it doesn't spill, but never remove it — because you don't want to. That's an incomplete response weighed down by attachment, and it lingers.

So the skill is to respond 100% without attachment: not over-responding, not under-responding, not bending your behavior around what you're clinging to. ("I want to leave my job but I owe my boss, so I stay too long" — that's attachment distorting response.) In medicine, you do everything appropriate for a patient and fully accept you can't control life and death; get caught up in "saving a life" and you'll do more harm than good. You can keep your enjoyment and even keep doing the action — you only give up the attachment. Board the train with eyes wide open, accept that it could end in any number of places, and then you get to enjoy the ride.

Spotting an Asura

A practical diagnostic. You can tell an asura (a being just above the human level on the conscious plane) is at work in someone's life by one signature:

Rapid, abnormal material advancement combined with paradoxical unhappiness.

When someone gets everything they asked for and grows more miserable, that's the tell. Asuras grant wishes like a malevolent genie — but the granting never satisfies. That's precisely why you don't work with them: they give you what you want and leave you worse off than before.

Common Pitfalls

  • Demanding proof. Proof and objectivity are features of the physical plane. Waiting for science to verify the consciousness plane before you'll explore it is like an anglerfish refusing to believe in the sun. You must discover it yourself, subjectively.
  • Treating "objective = real, subjective = fake." If facing a different direction feels different in meditation while the brain scan looks identical, the logical conclusion isn't "it's nothing." It's that there's a third variable outside the brain. Don't reflexively explain away every experience as placebo.
  • Chasing the fruit instead of tending the plant. The moment practice starts to bear fruit, people fixate on the fruit and neglect the plant that produced it. Keep watering the plant — for years more, long after results appear.
  • Letting expectation sabotage you. Expectation keeps the mind active, breeds disappointment, triggers a physiological reaction, and anchors your consciousness back in the body. The more you want an experience, the more your mind will manufacture a convincing fake of it. (This is why posting "I started astral traveling!" usually means it wasn't real.)
  • Mistaking the mudskipper for the destination. Plenty of capable meditators plateau at the surface, content to hover, never realizing it's only step one.
  • Confusing cause and effect. The Himalayas didn't produce great spirituality because of ethnicity or culture — fertile spiritual terrain fostered the traditions, just as fertile soil fosters farmers. Don't mythologize the people; respect the conditions.
  • Rushing. Spiritual progress is measured in lifetimes, and that only feels unbearable from inside a one-life frame.
  • Ego about understanding. If you grasp this material, it's because of a dozen prior moments of confusion that were necessary brushstrokes. If you don't grasp it, that confusion is the first stroke on a blank canvas. Neither is better.
