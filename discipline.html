Why You Don't Need to Win the Internal Battle

Discipline gets hard the moment a second thought appears: gym versus FF7. Now there's conflict.

Useless Action: The Mandala Practice

One hidden obstacle is your mind's drive to not be wasteful. Procrastination dresses itself as optimization: "I focus better in one long block… no point starting until I get the template back." Every excuse is efficiency-maxxing — and the paradox is that optimizing for productivity reliably produces procrastination. Wait forever for the perfect four hours and you defer forever.

Real discipline is come hell or high water — which means you must accept the L: accept that some effort will be "wasted," that you might take the test twice. Accepting the loss removes the loss, because it ends the procrastination. Train it with mandala practice — deliberate action with no payoff.
