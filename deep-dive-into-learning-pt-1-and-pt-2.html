  1. Understand the basics — grasp the core facts.
  2. Manipulate the information — play with it, recombine it.

Turning Fleeting Curiosity Into Lifelong Learning

People constantly get curious, buy the supplies, and let it collect dust three months later. The fix is understanding that interest develops through four phases — and you cannot skip them.

  1. Triggered situational interest. Curiosity sparked from the outside — a short video, an ad, something you saw. It's not a choice; it comes through your senses. This is where everyone starts.
  2. Sustained situational interest. Still externally driven, but now you need repeated exposure. This is the step people skip. Don't try to manufacture internal passion yet — just increase exposure. Curious about anime? Go to a convention. Hang around people who are into it. Let the environment keep feeding the spark.
