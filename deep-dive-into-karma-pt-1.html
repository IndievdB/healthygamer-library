Pain Is Not Suffering

A key distinction: pain and suffering are not the same thing. Neither are sadness and suffering.

  • Pain is a bodily signal or a raw emotion. Lifters chase "the pump" — muscles literally tearing — and love it.

So pain is often prarabda — it comes your way and you can't always control it. But suffering is what you add. The emotion, experienced fully, is fine — even healing. It's what your mind does with it that binds you.

The Final Paradox: Wanting No Attachment Is an Attachment

Wanting to have no karma is itself an attachment. That game can't be cleanly won. The resolution isn't to retreat from life but to plunge in fully:

"I will detach myself with attachment. I will let myself engage in the world. Let me flow down this river and get knocked around by whatever rocks are in it... That's what I signed up for."

You don't have to win enlightenment to play. The point isn't to stop playing the game of life; it's to fill your deck with good cards and play each hand fully without clinging to the result.
