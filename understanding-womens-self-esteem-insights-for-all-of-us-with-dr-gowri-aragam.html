Femininity, Masculinity, and Code-Switching Without Losing Yourself

A reframe that helps everyone: "feminine" and "masculine" describe energies that exist in all of us, not genders. Tying feminine to woman and masculine to man is a modern, socially constructed split. Sensitivity, tenderness, attunement — and immense strength — get labeled "feminine"; assertiveness and protection get labeled "masculine." But every person needs both, in different ratios, because life sometimes calls for softness and sometimes calls for fierceness.

The harm comes when these traits get artificially segregated by gender. A naturally empathetic man, told that empathy is "weak" or "girly," edits himself, shuts off his best quality, and may grow into the dad who barks "stop crying." A naturally assertive woman, told assertiveness is "off-putting," silences herself and lets things happen she doesn't want.

The antidote is to drop the labels and get to know who you actually are. Ask in the moment: Do I want to be assertive right now? Why? Am I sad, ashamed, anxious? Why? Getting to know yourself is itself proof that your inner experience has value.
