The Trap Hidden Inside Every Spiritual Question

Questions feel like the engine of learning, and in most domains they are. But spirituality is the one space where questions are often deliberately not answered.

Here's the mechanism. The moment someone hands you an answer, it creates an impression of understanding in your mind. Souls Travel in Packs

Souls tend to reincarnate in groups. Your heaviest karmic debts cluster among the people closest to you. Relationships flip across lifetimes to settle the books: this time the son, next time the father, until it evens out.

This is why you sometimes meet a stranger and feel an instant, inexplicable bond. The debt doesn't always require a lasting relationship — sometimes it's discharged in a single moment, even a nod between strangers:

"Anyone that you nod at, you know from a past life. There's a discharge — 'it's nice to see you again.'"

You needn't take it literally. But it gestures at a felt truth: connection often arrives faster and deeper than the few minutes you've known someone can explain.
